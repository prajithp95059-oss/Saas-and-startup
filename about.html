Our story

We build tools for people who move fast on little sleep.

Stacky started in a co-working space in 2021 because our own founding team was tired of gluing together six tools just to see if the business was healthy. Five years later, that same frustration is still the reason we ship every feature.

Mission

Give founders their time back.

The average startup runs on 14 disconnected tools by Series A. We think that number should be one. Stacky exists to remove the operational drag that keeps small teams from shipping — so the hours you save go straight back into the product and the customers who use it.

We're a remote-first team of 68 people across 14 countries, backed by operators who've scaled companies from zero to IPO. Every quarter we ship based on a simple rule: if a feature doesn't save a founder measurable time within 30 days, it doesn't ship.

Where we work

Three hubs, one remote-first culture.

Stacky has always been remote-first, but "remote" doesn't mean disconnected. We keep small physical hubs in Lisbon and Bengaluru for the teams who want a desk, plus a quarterly all-hands that brings the whole company together in person.

Everywhere else, the rule is simple: work happens async, decisions live in writing, and no one's timezone is treated as the default. It's how a 68-person team across 14 countries still ships every single week without burning out.

What we stand for

Six principles behind every decision we ship.

These aren't posters on a wall. They're the filter every roadmap decision runs through before a single line of code gets written.

01

Default to speed

We'd rather ship a good workflow this week than a perfect one next quarter. Momentum compounds — a rough edge you can iterate on beats a polished feature that arrives too late to matter.

02

Own the outcome

Every feature is judged by whether it moved a real metric for a real customer. If it doesn't, we rework it or cut it — shipping is the starting line, not the finish.

03

Write it down

Async by default. Decisions live in docs, not in someone's memory, so a teammate in Bengaluru can catch up on a call that happened in Lisbon overnight.

04

Stay reachable

Support is a founder-to-founder conversation, not a ticket queue. If you can find us on Slack, you skip the queue entirely.

05

Simplify ruthlessly

If a workflow needs a tutorial, we've already lost. Every new feature goes through a pass whose only job is to remove steps, not add them.

06

Ship in the open

Our roadmap, incident postmortems and changelog are public by default. Customers see what we're building — and why — before it ever ships.
